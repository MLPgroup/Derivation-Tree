The tension of the interface between the low temperature hadron phase and the high temperature quark-gluon plasma phase is an important parameter of the QCD phase transition. In the early universe the interface tension determines the nucleation rate of hadronic bubbles from the high temperature plasma. Thus, it sets the scale for spatial inhomogeneities in the baryon density, which may influence the primordial nucleosynthesis of light elements. To obtain an estimate for the interface tension in QCD we neglect the quarks and we concentrate on an S⁢U⁢(3)𝑆𝑈3italic-S italic-U OPEN:( NUMBER:3 CLOSE:)SU(3) pure glue theory.
