1 Introduction

Heavy quarkonium is a multiscale system covering all regimes of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) which make it an ideal place to study strong interactionsÂ [1]. Despite the success of QCD in high energy region, due to asymptotic freedom, non-perturbative effect dominates at low energies and brings problems to perturbative calculation. One tool to study this non-perturbative effect is lattice QCD. However, due to its huge calculation work, it is still unable to calculate all the physical quantities with the current computation power. Another important approach is to develop various phenomenological models. Among these phenomenological models, the quark model is a prominent one. Under the quark model framework, various types of interactions have been suggested by various groups, and they have achieved many impressive successes (see e.g. Refs.Â [2, 3, 4, 5, 6]). However, these potential models cannot be the whole story. One important missing ingredient is the mechanism to generate quark-antiquark pairs which enlarge the Fock space of the initial state, i.e. the initial state contains multiquark components. These multiquark components will change the Hamiltonian of the potential model, causing mass shift and mixing between states with the same quantum numbers or directly contributing to open channel strong decay if the initial state is above the corresponding threshold. These consequences can be summarized as unquenched effects or coupled-channel effects.

Through various approaches, such as P03superscriptsubscriptğ‘ƒ03FLOATSUPERSCRIPT:start NUMBER:3 FLOATSUPERSCRIPT:end italic-P POSTSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:0 POSTSUBSCRIPT:end{}^{3}P_{0} modelÂ [7, 8, 9, 10, 11], flux-tube breaking modelÂ [12, 13, 14, 15, 16], microscopic decay modelsÂ [4, 17, 18, 19], Sğ‘†italic-SS matrix analysisÂ [20, 21, 22, 23], coupled-channel effects are extensively studied in many literatures (e.g. Refs.Â [24, 25, 26, 27, 28])

4 Summary and Outlook

In this paper, under P03superscriptsubscriptğ‘ƒ03FLOATSUPERSCRIPT:start NUMBER:3 FLOATSUPERSCRIPT:end italic-P POSTSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:0 POSTSUBSCRIPT:end{}^{3}P_{0} model framework, we explicitly calculated the excited Bğµitalic-BB mesonsâ€™ contributions to the coupled-channel effects for the bottomonium. Since we do not fit our parameters with experimental data and we have exploit several different set of parameters, we have the enough reasons to believe that the difficulties are independent of the wave functions or the potential models.

We suggest that an efficient suppression mechanism such as dynamically suppression is needed to evaluate the coupled-channel effects. How to effectively sum up all the intermediate loops of coupled-channels still remains to be an open issue.
