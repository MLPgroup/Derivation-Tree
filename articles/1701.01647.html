Abstract

The original Oberbeck-Boussinesq(OB) equations which are the coupled two dimensional Navier-Stokes(NS) and heat conduction equations were investigated by E.N. Lorenz half a century ago with Fourier series and opened the way to the paradigm of chaos. In our former study - Chaos Solitons and Fractals 78, 249 (2015) - we presented fully analytic solutions for the velocity, pressure and temperature fields with the aim of the self-similar Ansatz and gave a possible explanation of the Rayleigh-BÃ¨nard convection cells. Now we generalize the Oberbeck-Boussinesq hydrodynamical system, going beyond the first order Boussinesq approximation or considering more general, power law dependent fluid viscosity or heat conduction material equations. Our analytic results obtained via the self-similar Ansatz may attract the interest of various fields like meteorology, oceanography or climate studies.

I Introduction

The investigation of coupled viscous flow equations to heat conduction has a fifteen-decade long history started with Boussinesq bous and Oberbeck ober who applied it to the normal atmosphere. At the beginning of the sixties - with the help of the stream function - Saltzman salz analyzed the problem with the help of finite Fourier series. At the same time Lorenz lorenz evaluated the numerical solutions with computers and plotted the first strange attractor which was the advent of chaos studies. A scientific history to this outstanding discovery can be found in the book of Gleick gleick . Lorenz and Salzman both transformed the original nonlinear partial differential equation (PDE) system to a coupled nonlinear ordinary differential equation (ODE) system via a truncated Fourier series. Investigating chaotic dynamical systems are still open up new questions and methods. In our former study barna we analyzed the original Boussinesq-Oberbeck PDE system with the self-similar Ansatz ending up with a non-linear ODE system, however the pressure, temperature and velocity field was evaluated in analytic forms with the help of the error functions. As main result we could see the possible birth of the Rayleigh-BÃ¨nard(RB) convection cell. Detailed physical description and exhausted technical details about the field of RB convection can be found in the books of ben1 ; ben2 ; ben3 . The connection of the self-similar Anasatz to critical phenomena, scaling, and renormalization was addressed as well. All our studies related to two or three dimensional Navier-Stokes equations analized with the self-similar Ansatz can be found in the book of Campos imre_book in Chapter 16. From our analytic velocity field with Fourier transformation additional connections to turbulence or enstropy could be evaluated as well. The references which are relevant to understand chaos and Lorenz attractor are presented in our above mentioned paper, therefore we skip it now.

In the present study we try to generalize this system considering more complicated material equations, like temperature dependent heat conduction coefficient, or viscosity. We go beyond the simplest Boussinesq approximation which couples heat conduction to fluid dynamics as well.

III Summary and Outlook

With reasonable generalization we investigated the classical OB equation which is the starting point of countless dynamical and chaotic systems. Instead of the usual Fourier truncation method we applied the two-dimensional generalization of the self-similar Ansatz and found a coupled non-linear ODE system which can be solved with quadrature. Our main result is that even this kind of generalization gave us an analytic temperature field which have some - not a single - oscillations. These oscillations could be the possible birth place of Rayleigh-BÃ¨nard convection cells. To our best knowledge certain parts of the climate models are based on the OB equations therefore our results might be an interesting sign to climate experts.
